What Every Florida Driver Should Know About the Point System


Understanding Florida’s Driver License Point System

Florida uses a point system to monitor and penalize drivers who commit traffic violations. Administered by the Florida Department of Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles (FLHSMV), this system assigns a specific number of points to your driving record for each moving violation. Accumulating too many points can lead to license suspension, higher insurance premiums, and long-term driving consequences. How Points Are Assigned for Traffic Violations

The number of points assigned depends on the severity of the offense. Here are common traffic infractions and their associated point values:

Three-Point Violations

  • Speeding 15 MPH or less over the posted limit

  • Driving with an open container of alcohol

  • Violating child restraint laws

  • Failure to obey traffic signals or signs

  • Improper lane change or turn

Four-Point Violations

  • Speeding more than 15 MPH over the posted limit

  • Passing a stopped school bus

  • Reckless driving

  • Running a red light

  • Failure to yield the right of way

Six-Point Violations

  • Leaving the scene of an accident with property damage over $50

  • Speeding resulting in an accident

  • Unlawful speed causing a crash

Each infraction stays on your record for at least three years from the date of the citation.

Consequences of Accumulating Too Many Points

License Suspension Thresholds

If you accrue too many points within specific timeframes, your driver’s license will be suspended:

  • 12 points within 12 months: 30-day suspension

  • 18 points within 18 months: 3-month suspension

  • 24 points within 36 months: 1-year suspension

These thresholds are strictly enforced. Points are cumulative, meaning if you continue committing violations, you will quickly exceed the limits.

Financial Impact

Insurance providers in Florida access your driving record. Accumulating points can lead to:

  • Increased insurance premiums

  • Difficulty qualifying for preferred insurance rates

  • Potential policy cancellation for high-risk drivers

How to Check Your Driving Record in Florida

You can view your point total and citation history through the FLHSMV’s online driver license check portal. This allows you to monitor your record and correct any errors.

Steps to Check Online

  1. Visit flhsmv.gov

  2. Navigate to “Driver License Check”

  3. Enter your driver license number

  4. Review your current license status and point total

Reducing Points on Your Record

While points remain on your record for three years, there are ways to mitigate their impact:

Traffic School (Basic Driver Improvement Course)

Eligible drivers can elect to attend a state-approved traffic school to:

  • Avoid points being added to their record

  • Prevent insurance rates from increasing

  • Maintain their safe driver designation

You can only use this option once every 12 months and a maximum of five times in your lifetime.

Contest the Citation

If you believe a ticket was issued in error, you have the legal right to contest it in court. If successful, no points will be added to your record. Hiring a traffic attorney can improve your chances of a favorable outcome.

Drivers Under 18: Stricter Penalties

Minors are subject to more stringent rules under Florida’s point system:

  • Six points within 12 months results in a restriction to “Business Purposes Only” driving for 12 months or until the driver turns 18

  • Any alcohol-related offense results in an automatic suspension, even if the BAC is below 0.08

Habitual Traffic Offender (HTO) Designation

A driver can be classified as an HTO if they accumulate three major offenses or 15 convictions for moving violations within five years. This leads to a five-year license revocation, and reinstatement requires:

  • Mandatory completion of advanced driver improvement courses

  • Payment of all fines and reinstatement fees

  • Potential re-testing

Key Takeaways for Florida Drivers

  • Always be aware of how many points you have on your license

  • Understand that even minor violations contribute to long-term consequences

  • Use traffic school to prevent point accumulation when possible

  • Drive defensively to avoid citations and potential license suspension

Understanding and managing your standing under Florida’s point system is essential to maintaining your driving privileges, minimizing financial penalties, and staying safe on the road.
